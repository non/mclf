Bio

Tiny Short Med Long Blah

Marie LeBlanc Flanagan is an artist working in the playful spaces between people, especially related to connection and community. Marie builds experimental video games, playful installations, and cooperative experiences and has an enduring fondness for the possibilities of trash.

Marie co-founded Wyrd Arts Initiatives; founded Drone Day; served as the editor-in-chief of Weird Canada; co-founded Imaginary Residency, an artist-run online residency; co-founded Toronto Games Week; and co-organizes GAIA.

Marie has worked with Wizard Zines, A MAZE. Berlin International Games and Playful Media Festival, The Processing Foundation, The School of Machines, Making, and Make-Believe, Ada x, Daily Tous Les Jours, and Game Arts International Network.



Timeline

2023 2022 2021 2020 2019

Whee! I co-founded Toronto Games Week, a celebration of playable arts and culture with Jim Munroe. We celebrated the 9th annual Drone Day. I co-organized the GAIA Symposium, an international think tank and network for the professional development of the game arts community: aimed at curators, producers, event organizers and academics working in the field of videogame culture. I wrote and illustrated If you don't like the game, change the rules, a comic about co-ops, unions, and work in the games industry. I travelled to Mexico City for OASIS CENART in Ciudad de Mexico thanks to the Canadian Embassy and SAE Institute Mexico and had the chance to talk about my games and research into games and work. I hosted a collaborative zine-making workshop and Artist Panel x Labour Rights in Halifax with Eyelevel Gallery. I paired with Julia Evans to create How Integers and Floats Work (floats are tricky!). I did a DIY citizen science retreat with Lee Cyborg, Hillary Predko, Julia Evans, and company. I delightedly explored the hills of Pittsburgh during an artist residency at the Entertainment Technology Center at Carnegie Mellon University in Pittsburgh and created a game-poem with Heather Kelley and other collaborators that we showed at LIKELIKE. I travelled to Point Arena and Los Angeles to intentionally fall into the ocean and make zines, animation flipbooks, and a whale fall with Cabbibo and Pen Ward. The year is not done yet!
