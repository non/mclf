Long

Marie LeBlanc Flanagan is an artist working in the playful spaces between people, especially related to connection and community. Marie builds playful installations, experimental video games, and cooperative experiences. Marie works with experimental gameplay, cooperation, virtual and augmented realities, systems, narrative, open source data, biosignals, smell art, sonic art, computer vision, machine, and human learning.

Marie has shown work all over the world, including Brasil, South Africa, Ukraine, UK, and the USA and has completed residencies at the NYU Game Center, Technoculture, Art and Games, Metalab - The Society for Arts and Technology, Softie Feelies, and The LIVELab,

Marie started Wyrd Arts Initiatives a nonprofit dedicated to encouraging and documenting creative expression across Canada and Drone Day, an international day for the celebration of experimental drone music and communities.

Marie has worked with a number of artists and arts organizations including Wizard Zines, A MAZE. Berlin International Games and Playful Media Festival, Ada X, The School of Machines, Making, and Make-Believe, Ada X, Genielab, Daily Tous Les Jours, and Long Winter.

Marie is on the board of Inter Arts Matrix, is on the Eastern Bloc artistic programming committee; and has served as a Polaris Prize Juror.

Marie co-organized GAIA 2021, a 9-session online conference for 150+ game curators around the world and cocreated the Imaginary Residency, an artist-run online residency.
